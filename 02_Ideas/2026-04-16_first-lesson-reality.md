## Core idea

Many adults who are curious about guitar lessons imagine either a rigid classical method ("hold the neck like this") or something vague ("just play along and have fun"). Neither is right. A realistic, specific description of what a first lesson with Humberto actually looks like addresses the anxiety and uncertainty that stops people from inquiring.

## Angle suggestion

Walk through an actual first lesson structure — not as a pitch, but as an honest description. What gets assessed. What gets started. What a student leaves with. No promises. No hype.

## Key details to include

- Assessment of where the student is (not a test — a conversation)
- What we'd work on in session 1 (depends on level, but give two scenarios: beginner and someone who's played a bit)
- What the student would be able to do or understand by the end
- No performance requirements, no prior theory required

## Possible CTA

Direct: "If you're looking for guitar lessons in St. John's, message me. Taking new students."

## Notes

This is one of the best organic lead-generation posts we can make — it's genuinely useful, not promotional-sounding, and it directly addresses the uncertainty that keeps people from reaching out. Brief should keep it short (5–8 lines max). Resist the urge to describe everything.
