## Core idea

When a student achieves something concrete in a lesson, that moment is worth sharing. Not as self-promotion — the focus should be entirely on the student and the achievement. These posts are some of the most effective for attracting new students because they make the abstract ("will I improve?") concrete.

## Angle suggestion

A specific moment: what the student was working on, what clicked, what it sounded like when it worked. No names. No identifying details. The skill or musical moment is the story.

## Key details to include

- The specific thing the student achieved (playing a ii-V-I in time, reading a melody off the page for the first time, getting the groove of a samba pattern right, etc.)
- Why that moment matters (what it means for their playing going forward)
- How long they had been working on it (optional — gives scale)

## PRIVACY RULE

**No names. No ages. No identifying details whatsoever.**
If the student is part of the Jazz Combo, do not distinguish individual students — write about the group result only.
