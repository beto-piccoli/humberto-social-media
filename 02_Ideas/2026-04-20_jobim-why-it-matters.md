## Core idea

Antônio Carlos Jobim wrote music that sounds simple and is not. Playing it well is a particular kind of discipline — it demands taste, restraint, and a deep knowledge of Brazilian harmony. For a guitarist who came up playing this music, explaining what Jobim means to them is a window into their entire musical identity.

## Angle suggestion

Not a biography of Jobim — everyone can Google that. Humberto's personal relationship with the music. One specific Jobim piece or element that illustrates something he values. Why playing Jobim is harder than it sounds.

## Key details to include

- One specific Jobim tune or harmonic idea that matters to Humberto
- What it revealed to him about the guitar or music in general
- Why it's still relevant — what it still teaches

## Possible CTA

None, or: "Come hear it live sometime."
