## Core idea

Most people who know something about Brazilian music know bossa nova. Far fewer know choro — and yet choro is one of the richest and most technically demanding guitar traditions in the world. It predates bossa nova by decades. It's also deeply connected to how Humberto thinks about the guitar.

A short post explaining what choro is — not the Wikipedia version, but Humberto's version, from someone who grew up with it — would be genuinely interesting to jazz and music fans.

## Angle suggestion

Start with what it sounds like rather than its history. The counterpoint between melody and bass line. The way rhythm works. What it demands from the player. One specific piece or moment that illustrates it.

## Key details to include

- What choro is (briefly, in plain language)
- What makes it distinct from bossa nova
- One specific example: a classic piece or player that demonstrates the style
- Why Humberto plays it and what it teaches

## Possible CTA

None, or: "If you're curious — come out when I play it live, or look up [specific recording]."

## Notes

This should read like Humberto explaining something he loves to a curious person at a gig. Not a music theory lecture. Not a history lesson. A conversation starter.
