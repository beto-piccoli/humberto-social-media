## Core idea

The ii-V-I progression is the most common harmonic movement in jazz, and it's also the most misunderstood by students. Most people learn to name it. Fewer understand how to actually use it — what to do when one arrives, how to voice it, how to feel it before you see it on a chart.

A post about what teaching the two-five-one in a real musical context looks like would serve two purposes: it demonstrates depth and approach, and it's practically useful to anyone learning jazz.

## Angle suggestion

Focus on the gap between knowing the name and being able to use it. One specific example from teaching. Not a theory lecture — a moment from a real lesson.

## Key details to include

- The progression by name (G-7 → C7 → FΔ in a real key, not abstract Roman numerals)
- What it sounds like when a student first plays it correctly in context
- Why context matters — hearing it in a tune vs. drilling it in isolation
