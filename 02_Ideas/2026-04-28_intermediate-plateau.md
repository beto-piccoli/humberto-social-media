## Core idea

There's a very specific point where guitar players get stuck — usually after 2–3 years of self-teaching or informal learning. They can play some things, but they feel like they've stopped improving. They don't know what to work on. They can play songs but can't improvise. They know a few chords but don't understand why they work.

Naming this plateau specifically — and showing that there's a clear path through it — is the single most effective thing you can say to the audience that most needs guitar lessons.

## Angle suggestion

Describe the plateau specifically. Name the symptoms: playing the same songs on repeat, not being able to improvise, knowing chord shapes but not understanding harmony. Then one concrete direction out of it — not a full lesson plan, just one specific thing that changes.

## Key details to include

- A specific description of where the plateau is (it should read like a mirror for people in that situation)
- What causes it (usually: learning by tab or YouTube shortcuts, no theory foundation, no ear training)
- One specific thing that starts to break the pattern
- Optional: an example from a student who was in this spot

## Possible CTA

Soft/direct bridge: "If this sounds familiar, private lessons can help you move past it. Message me."

## Notes

This is the highest-leverage content idea in the lessons campaign. The plateau is real and specific — anyone who has been stuck here will recognize themselves immediately. The brief should push Maya to name the plateau very specifically. Avoid: "everyone gets stuck sometimes." That's not good enough. Name what actually happens.
