# Audience Profiles
_Owner: Nico (Content Strategist)_
_Last updated: 2026-04-10_

---

## Overview

These are the primary audience types for Humberto's social media. Different content serves different audiences. Understanding who you're talking to changes what you say and how you say it.

---

## Audience A — The Curious Adult Learner

**Who they are:** Adults in St. John's (roughly 30–55) who have always been interested in guitar but never committed, or who started as teenagers and dropped it. Some music background, but not advanced.

**What they want:** To actually learn — not just dabble. They're looking for a teacher who is serious without being intimidating.

**What catches their attention:**
- Short, specific posts about what a real lesson covers
- Student results (without being promotional)
- Content that makes jazz or Brazilian music feel accessible
- Seeing that you don't need to be a prodigy to learn

**What puts them off:**
- Elitism (implied or explicit)
- Overselling
- Jargon without explanation

**Content types that work:**
- Teaching observations
- Student milestones (anonymized)
- "What I teach and why" reflections
- Behind-the-scenes of lesson prep

---

## Audience B — The Existing Music Community (St. John's)

**Who they are:** Local musicians, music educators, music fans, Jazz East members, people already in the St. John's arts scene.

**What they want:** To stay connected to the local scene, to know what's happening, to see who's doing interesting work.

**What catches their attention:**
- Gig announcements
- Community events (Jazz East, local shows)
- Conversations about jazz or Brazilian music
- Behind-the-scenes of real projects
- Collaborations with other local musicians

**What puts them off:**
- Anything that feels like self-promotion disconnected from actual work
- Generic inspiration content
- Ignoring the local scene context

**Content types that work:**
- Gig posts
- Rehearsal process content
- Collaborator shoutouts
- Jazz East news
- Local music commentary (when relevant)

---

## Audience C — Jazz and Brazilian Music Enthusiasts (Broader)

**Who they are:** Not necessarily local. People who follow jazz guitar, Brazilian music, or niche music education content.

**What they want:** Content that speaks their language — specific enough to be credible.

**What catches their attention:**
- Specific repertoire mentions (Jobim, Shorter, Jobim arrangement approaches)
- Technique and theory observations
- Brazilian music context (choro, samba rhythmic nuance, etc.)

**What puts them off:**
- Oversimplified or clickbaity framing of complex music topics

**Content types that work:**
- Short music insights
- Repertoire deep dives
- Teaching methodology thoughts
- Practice reflections

---

## Audience D — Potential Grant Funders / Arts Organizations

**Who they are:** Arts councils, grant reviewers, festival programmers, arts organization members.

**What they want:** Evidence of active professional work, community engagement, artistic identity.

**What catches their attention:**
- Consistent, serious presence
- Community contribution content
- Documentation of real projects
- Clear artistic identity

**Content types that work:**
- Project documentation
- Teaching program highlights
- Local collaborations
- Reflections on the role of jazz/Brazilian music in the community

---

## Primary focus for Facebook

Audiences A and B are the core Facebook audience at this stage. Content should speak primarily to them.

Audiences C and D benefit from the same content but are secondary in calibration.
