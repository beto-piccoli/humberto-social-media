# Content Pillars — Humberto Ramos Social Media
_Owner: Nico_
_Last updated: 2026-04-10_

---

## What content pillars are

Pillars are the recurring themes that organize all content. They prevent the feed from being random and ensure that each platform consistently communicates what Humberto does and who he is. They also make it easy to plan — when you're not sure what to post, pick a pillar.

Each post belongs to exactly one primary pillar (logged in `content_items.pillar`).

---

## The 9 Pillars

---

### 1. Live Performance

**What it covers:**
Upcoming gigs, gig recaps, show announcements, venue posts, touring (if applicable), festival appearances, concerts.

**Goal:**
Drive attendance. Document Humberto's active performance career. Build the association: "Humberto is a working gigging musician."

**Typical content:**
- Gig announcement: date, venue, who's playing, what to expect
- Day-of post: short, warm, a small detail about the set
- Post-gig recap: what happened, one specific moment worth sharing
- "We're at [venue] tonight" (if posting in the moment)

**Tone:** Warm, inviting, specific. Not hype.

**CTA examples:**
- "Come out if you're around."
- "Tickets at [link]." / "Free admission — [venue], [time]."
- "Hope to see some of you there."

---

### 2. Guitar Teaching

**What it covers:**
What Humberto teaches, how he teaches it, what students work on, what good lessons look like, and invitations to study with him.

**Goal:**
Attract new students. Build trust with potential students who are researching teachers. Show depth without intimidating.

**Typical content:**
- A concept explained briefly and clearly
- What a student worked on this week (anonymized)
- A common student challenge and how to approach it
- Why Humberto teaches what he teaches
- Practical tips (short, specific, useful)

**Tone:** Clear and direct. Accessible without being oversimplified. Authoritative without being intimidating.

**CTA examples:**
- "Taking new students. Message me if you're looking for lessons."
- "Spots available for private guitar lessons. DM for details."

---

### 3. Jazz and Brazilian Music Education

**What it covers:**
Repertoire knowledge, style insights, music theory observations, historical context, cultural background of jazz and Brazilian music. Content that positions Humberto as a knowledgeable voice in these idioms.

**Goal:**
Build credibility with music-knowledgeable audiences. Create shareable content for jazz and Brazilian music communities. Establish voice as a genuine specialist, not just someone who plays these styles.

**Typical content:**
- Short observation about a specific tune or style element
- Something interesting about choro, bossa nova, or jazz harmony
- A recording recommendation with context
- A brief reflection on a performance practice or improvisation concept

**Tone:** Thoughtful, specific. Assumes some music background but doesn't exclude curious non-musicians.

**CTA examples:**
- Usually soft or none: "Listen to [recording] — it's worth it."
- "If you're interested in this kind of thing, I teach guitar lessons in St. John's — message me."

---

### 4. Behind the Scenes / Rehearsal Process

**What it covers:**
What rehearsal, practice, and preparation actually looks like. Not the polished performance — the work behind it.

**Goal:**
Humanize the work. Show discipline and craft. Create a connection between audience and the music they'll hear.

**Typical content:**
- "Working on X for the show this week — this section keeps catching me"
- A practice method or routine note
- Rehearsal with other musicians (with permission)
- Notation, charts, or study materials (a photo of a score, a chord chart)
- "Before the gig" moments

**Tone:** Real and unpretentious. Not curated inspiration — actual work.

**CTA examples:**
- Usually no explicit CTA — this pillar builds relationship, not action
- Soft: "Come hear the result on [date] at [venue]."

---

### 5. Personal Artist Identity

**What it covers:**
Who Humberto is as a musician. His influences, his perspective on music, what he finds interesting, why he plays what he plays. The connection between his Brazilian background and his life in Newfoundland. Reflections on the musician's life.

**Goal:**
Build a sense of person, not just professional. The audience should feel like they know something real about him.

**Typical content:**
- A reflection on a musician who influenced him (named, specific)
- An observation about why a particular piece of music matters to him
- A thought about what it's like to play jazz or Brazilian music in St. John's
- A moment of honest perspective on the musician's life (practice, uncertainty, breakthroughs)

**Tone:** Genuine, unhurried, personal. Not inspirational poster content.

**CTA examples:**
- Rarely — this pillar is about presence, not action
- Soft only: "What are you listening to lately? I'm curious."

---

### 6. Community / Local Music Scene

**What it covers:**
The St. John's music community. Jazz East. Other local musicians and events. The arts scene in Newfoundland. Events worth knowing about.

**Goal:**
Position Humberto as part of the community, not just a person promoting themselves. Builds goodwill with local musicians and arts organizations.

**Typical content:**
- Jazz East concert or event announcement
- Shoutout to another local musician's show or release (genuinely)
- Community arts news relevant to the music scene
- Reflection on what the local scene offers

**Tone:** Community-minded, generous, not self-promotional in the traditional sense.

**CTA examples:**
- "Come out." / "Worth seeing."
- For Jazz East: specific event info.

---

### 7. Student Results / Social Proof

**What it covers:**
Student milestones, teaching program outcomes, what students achieve after working with Humberto. Always anonymized unless the student explicitly gives permission.

**Goal:**
Show that lessons produce real results. The most effective content for attracting new students.

**Typical content:**
- "A student nailed [specific skill] today for the first time."
- "End of session — this group went from struggling with [X] to playing [Y]."
- A milestone moment from the Jazz Combo

**Tone:** Focused on the student, not on Humberto's teaching. The student's result is the story.

**Privacy rules:**
- No student names
- No identifiable details without explicit written permission
- Combo is a group — group results (not individual) are generally OK

---

### 8. Offers and Invitations

**What it covers:**
Direct, clear posts about Humberto's services. Private lessons, group programs, upcoming enrollment windows.

**Goal:**
Convert awareness into action. These are the posts where you actually ask someone to become a student or come to a gig.

**Typical content:**
- "Taking new students for private guitar lessons." (direct enrollment post)
- "Spring session spots available — message me."
- "If you've been thinking about starting guitar lessons..."

**Tone:** Direct and unpretentious. No high-pressure language. One clear ask.

**Frequency limit:** No more than 1 out of every 5–6 posts should be a direct offer. Surrounded by other pillars, offers feel natural. Isolated, they feel like ads.

---

### 9. Thoughtful Reflections on Music and Practice

**What it covers:**
Observations about the practice of music, the experience of learning, what musicians discover over time. Philosophical without being vague. Grounded in real musical experience.

**Goal:**
Depth and differentiation. This is where Humberto's voice comes through most clearly as a thoughtful musician and educator — not just a teacher posting tips.

**Typical content:**
- "Something I've noticed about how students plateau at the same place..."
- "There's a version of jazz guitar that's all about knowing the right chords. I've become less interested in that version."
- "What it actually takes to play in time — not what people say it takes"

**Tone:** Reflective, first person, unhurried. Can be a longer post if the thought earns it.

**CTA examples:**
- Usually none. Let the reflection be complete.
- Soft only: "Curious if others notice this."

## Pillar balance targets

Per 10 posts, approximate distribution:
| Pillar | Target % | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Guitar Teaching | 20% | Core audience driver |
| Live Performance | 15% | Varies with gig schedule |
| Jazz & Brazilian Education | 15% | Credibility content |
| Behind the Scenes | 10% | Process / humanizing |
| Personal Artist Identity | 10% | Voice / depth |
| Community / Local Scene | 10% | Goodwill |
| Student Results | 8% | Social proof |
| Offers and Invitations | 7% | Conversion — no more than this |
| Thoughtful Reflections | 5% | Depth / differentiation |

These are guidelines. Adjust to what's actually happening. If you have 3 gigs in a month, performance content goes up. If enrollment is open, offers go up temporarily. Pillars flex around real life.
